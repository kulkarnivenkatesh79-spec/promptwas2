Step by Step

The Election Process

📋
Phase 01

Voter Registration

Citizens enroll on official electoral rolls before a set deadline, verifying identity and eligibility.

Typically opens 6–12 months before election day. Citizens must be 18+, provide proof of residence, and submit identity documents. Online portals (like India's Voter Helpline App) make this simpler. Deadlines are strictly enforced — register early!

🗳️
Phase 02

Candidate Filing

Political parties and independents officially declare candidacy by filing nomination papers.

Candidates must meet eligibility criteria (age, citizenship, no criminal convictions), pay a security deposit, and file a declaration of assets. The Election Commission reviews submissions and publishes the final candidate list.
open_in_new ECI Official Portal
📢
Phase 03

Campaign Period

Candidates actively campaign — rallies, debates, social media, and outreach to win voter support.

Campaigns are regulated by election authorities to ensure fair play. Spending limits apply, hate speech is prohibited, and a "silent period" (48 hours before voting) restricts active campaigning. Debates are organised for public scrutiny of candidates' policies.

04
🗳️
Phase 04

Voting Day

Registered voters cast ballots at designated polling booths using paper ballots or EVMs.

Polls open from 7am–6pm typically. Voters show ID, receive a ballot, vote privately, and deposit it. Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) count automatically. International observers may monitor proceedings. Ink is applied to a finger to prevent double-voting.
open_in_new ECI Official Website
🔢
Phase 05

Vote Counting

Ballots are counted under strict supervision with party agents present to verify the count.

EVM counts are tallied constituency-by-constituency. Results are declared round by round. Candidates or agents may request a recount if margins are thin. Final results are certified and reported to the Election Commission.
play_circle Watch: How Votes Are Counted
05
06
🏆
Phase 06

Results Declaration

The Election Commission officially announces winners and losers for each constituency.

Results are published on official government portals and transmitted to broadcasters. The winning party or coalition must demonstrate a majority to form government. In case of hung parliament, coalition negotiations begin.
open_in_new ECI Results Portal
🏛️
Phase 07

Government Formation

The winning leader is invited to form government, takes the oath of office, and begins their term.

The Head of State invites the majority party/coalition leader to form a government. The new Cabinet is sworn in, ministries are allocated, and the new government's 100-day agenda is set. Parliamentary sessions resume.
